Background
___
In India, mental health is a domain that remains largely ignored by people due to a variety of reasons. All Ears is an early stage startup on a mission to promote mental wellbeing. We are trying to understand the Indian context and explore the possibilities of catering to mental health issues using technology.
Research and insights
What are the problems in Indian context?
___
Currently, two polar options exist to help open people up and talk about feelings. First, theraphy sessions, which are expensive and still have an associated social stigma. Second, online peer-to-peer chat platforms, which are unmoderated and are often sources of random unsolicited comments.
01
Mental health is not thought as a problem to be solved
02
Most people do not understand how therapy or listening can help
03
Number of psychology professionals exceeds number of people seeking help
04
Psychology graduates are willing to become empathetic listeners to get practical experience
Goal
___
To make mental health support and services inclusive and accessible using technology
Version 1
All Ears app
___
1
Anonymity: People who need someone to listen can anonymously talk about their feelings and issues.
2
Temporary chats: Holding a conversation and consistent exchange of thoughts is essential, and hence the chats are temporary.
3
Listening: Empaths are psychology experts willing to listen and provide mental health support.
4
Trust & community: Social profile of empaths helps in maintaining trust, with badges about their listening skills.
Testing
Beta Testing and Pivot Point
___
The beta version of the app was tested with 60 participants from different psychology colleges.
Aim: To understand if people are willing to share and open up in the presence of empathetic listeners, and if they are willing to pay a meagre amount.
Result: This activity led us to learn that people in India do not consider dealing with mental health issues as something worth paying, even for a small amount.

Due to unsustainable model, All Ears is pivoting in a direction that approaches mental wellbeing more broadly.
Reflection
What did I learn?
___
1
Real world challenges: There is a huge difference between designing a concept and designing a practical, real world application that will be used by people. Thinking about all kind of use cases and user flows during the design process is critical.
2
Collaboration with other stakeholders: I am working with co-founders, developers, and graphic designers on this project. Interacting with all of them during ideation and design hand-off have taught me the importance of soft skills.
