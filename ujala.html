Ujala
Helping the residents of BITS Goa during power cuts
Background
___
BITS Pilani K K Birla Goa Campus faces the problem of power outages sometimes, and most often, the outage affects only a certain area of the campus. In times of need, such as examination period, it may be difficult to find which area in the campus has power.
Challenge
How can we help deliver the right information to the people in real-time?
___
Solution
___
Ujala - A mobile app that provides accurate information by displaying the areas of campus that have power supply on a map.
Phase 1
How can we collect information about power supply and display the same in real-time?
___
Crowdsourcing
Based on the analysis of charging patterns, we concluded that different people charge their phones at different times. Interestingly, there is a significantly high chance that at least a few mobile devices are getting charged in a given area at any point of time. Hence, we could crowdsource this data because charging devices would indicate the presence of power supply.
Running the app in background
To get real-time crowdsourced data, developing the app to run in background was crucial. Ideally, people would open the app only when they need to know which part of the campus has power. But, we need to gather data all the time, and hence the app must run in background to monitor the charging state of mobile.
The map view
Since the app is based on the concept of crowdsourcing, we wanted as many people to use it as possible, because more users would mean more data points, resulting in much more reliable information. Displaying the information in a useful and visually appealing manner would encourage users to keep using the app. So we decided to show pointers that indicate the presence of power supply on a map view, which would make it easier for people to understand.
Testing
After the development of the first version of the app, we conducted an intensive testing of the app with real users and received feedback from around 50 on-campus residents. Though the feedback regarding the functionality and usefulness of the app was positive, we also discovered that user experience was not as per the expectations.
Phase 2
How can we improve the experience of users on Ujala?
___
UI revamp
In phase 1, the app was developed with a very basic UI which was not so appealing. In order to provide a better user experience, we revamped the UI of Ujala in phase 2. With a fresh font, colour scheme and icons, the app had a new charm altogether in terms of look and feel. Dark theme was also implemented as a part of this process.
Onboarding
From the initial feedback that we received during our testing phase, many users indicated that they did not understand what the app was and how to use it. To help users understand the app and its usage, we designed onboarding screens, explaining key features of the app.
Supplementary features
We further added some supplementary features to help the users take relevant actions right from the app settings page. Options like changing the app theme, providing feedback, inviting others to use the app, etc. were now all present in a single place.
Branding & identity
Every mobile app has an icon associated with it, which represents the app in a graphical manner. An app is recognised by the users through its icon. A new icon for Ujala was designed, keeping in mind the use cases. Since the app is focused on the people of BITS Pilani K K Birla Goa Campus, a component of the BITS logo was inculcated in the form of tricolour pattern in a shredded circle. A minimalistic graphic of bulb was included to represent the connection with electric power.
